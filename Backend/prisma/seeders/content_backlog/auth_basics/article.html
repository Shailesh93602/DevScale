Understanding Authentication Basics

Using **Authentication Basics** correctly to manage your user's identities is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What are Authentication Basics?

Authentication basics include the process of correctly and securely verifying exactly who a user is—such as through its **Passwords**, its **MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)**, and its **Biometrics**—as intended.

2. Important Factors

Authentication factors—including **Something you know** (passwords), **Something you have** (tokens), and **Something you are** (fingerprints)—provide powerful, integrated, and feature-rich security for your project's application.

Conclusion

By making authentication basics a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
