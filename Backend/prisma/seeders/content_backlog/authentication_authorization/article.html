Understanding Authentication and Authorization

Using **Authentication** and **Authorization** correctly to manage your project's user access is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Authentication?

Authentication is the process of correctly and securely verifying exactly who a user is—such as through its **Login** and its **Password**—ensuring only authorized users can access your project's application.

2. What is Authorization?

Authorization is the process of correctly and securely verifying exactly what an authenticated user is and is not allowed to do—such as through its **User Roles** and its **Permissions**—within your software project as intended.

Conclusion

By making authentication and authorization a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
