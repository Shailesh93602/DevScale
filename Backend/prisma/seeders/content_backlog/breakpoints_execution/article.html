Understanding Breakpoints and Step-by-Step Execution

Using the powerful, integrated, and feature-rich development tools provided by modern browsers is critical for developing high-quality web applications. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What are Breakpoints?

Breakpoints are a custom status in your web application's code that tells the browser to pause its execution at a specific line, allowing you to manually inspect and debug its current state.

2. Step-by-Step Execution

Once your application's code has been paused by a breakpoint, you can manually step through each line of code, observing exactly how its status and data changes at each step.

3. Choosing a Debugging Strategy

Choose a debugging strategy that works for you and your team, such as using breakpoints to identify the cause of complex logic errors or using the console to view status messages.

Conclusion

By making breakpoints a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
