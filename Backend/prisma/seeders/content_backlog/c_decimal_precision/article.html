Understanding Decimal Precision in C

Decimal precision refers to the number of digits after the decimal point when representing floating-point numbers (floats and doubles) in C.

1. Floats and Doubles

  • Float: Typically has about 7 decimal digits of precision.
  • Double: Usually has about 15 decimal digits of precision, making it more accurate for complex calculations.

2. Handling Inaccuracies

Floating-point numbers are represented in binary internally, which means they can't accurately represent some decimal values (e.g., 0.1). This can lead to small rounding errors.

3. Output Formatting

You can control the number of decimal places displayed when printing using Format Specifiers. For example, %.2f will print a float with exactly two decimal places.

Conclusion

Understanding precision is crucial when performing financial calculations or scientific simulations, where even tiny inaccuracies can lead to large mistakes.
