Understanding C Operators

Operators are symbols that tell the compiler to perform specific mathematical or logical manipulations on operands (variables and values).

1. Arithmetic Operators

Used to perform basic math: + (Addition), - (Subtraction), * (Multiplication), / (Division), and % (Modulus - remainder of a division).

2. Relational and Logical Operators

Relational: Compare two values (e.g., ==, !=, >, <). Logical: Combine conditions using && (AND), || (OR), and ! (NOT).

3. Assignment Operators

Used to assign values to variables. C provides both simple assignment (=) and compound assignments (like +=, -=, *=).

Conclusion

Operators are the tools you use to transform data and make logical decisions within your code. Mastering their usage and precedence is key to writing effective C programs.
