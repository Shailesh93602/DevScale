Understanding Continuous Deployment Strategies

Using the powerful, integrated, and feature-rich development strategies provided by modern DevOps platforms, you can create a suite of unit tests for your project. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Continuous Deployment (CD)?

Continuous Deployment is a software development methodology that automatically deploys every code change that passes your project's automated tests directly into your production environment, ensuring your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and organized for your users.

2. Blue-Green Deployment

Blue-green deployment is a release strategy that uses two identical production environments (Blue and Green) to allow for zero-downtime updates and easy version rollbacks for your software.

3. Canary Releases

Canary release is a strategy that slowly rolls out a new version of your software to a small percentage of your users first, allowing you to identify and fix any errors before a full deployment occurs.

Conclusion

By making continuous deployment a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
