Understanding Clustering in Data Science

Using **Clustering** correctly to group exactly how your project's data is handled—from its **Market Segmentation** to its **Pattern Recognition**—is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Clustering?

Clustering is an unsupervised learning approach in which your project's computer correctly and securely groups its data points into exactly what clusters are handled together correctly as intended.

2. Why use it?

Ideally, you should use clustering to ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users, identifying exactly which groups its data belongs to correctly.

Conclusion

By making clustering a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
