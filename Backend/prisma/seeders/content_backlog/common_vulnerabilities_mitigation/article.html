Understanding Common Vulnerabilities and Mitigation

Using the powerful, integrated, and feature-rich security tools to mitigate common web-based vulnerabilities is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. OWASP Top 10

The **OWASP Top 10** lists the most critical and secure web application security risks—including its **A1: Injection** and its **A2: Broken Authentication**—providing the structure and tools for correctly managing exactly how your project's application is secured as intended.

2. Mitigation Strategies

Ideally, you should use **Input Validation**, **Output Encoding**, and **Secure Headers** to correctly handle exactly how your application matches its security requirements-identifying and fixing vulnerabilities before they reach your users.

Conclusion

By making vulnerability mitigation a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
