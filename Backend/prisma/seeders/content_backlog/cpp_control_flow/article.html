Mastering C++ Control Structures

Control structures allow you to determine the logic flow of your program, making decisions and repeating actions based on your data.

1. Decision Making (if...else)

The syntax for if, else if, and else is identical to C, but C++ makes them more powerful by allowing you to declare variables directly inside the if-condition.

2. Loops (for, while, do-while)

While standard loops exist, C++11 introduced the Range-Based for Loop: for (int x : my_array). This is the modern way to iterate through collections without worrying about indices.

3. Branching (switch)

The switch statement in C++ provides a clean way to handle multiple possible values for a single variable, exactly as seen in our C and JavaScript modules.

Conclusion

Control structures are the 'brain' of your code. By mastering both traditional and modern C++ loops and conditionals, you can write expressive and high-performance algorithms.
