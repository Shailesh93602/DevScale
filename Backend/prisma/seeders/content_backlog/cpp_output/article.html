C++ Output: Mastering cout

In C++, the cout object (pronounced "C-out") is the standard way to output or print text to the screen.

1. The Insertion Operator (<<)

To send data to the screen, we use cout together with the Insertion Operator (<<). You can chain multiple items together: cout << "Score: " << score;.

2. Ending Lines (endl vs \n)

To move to a new line, you can use the familiar \n or the C++ specific endl object. endl is special because it also "flushes the buffer", ensuring the text appears on the screen immediately.

3. Outputting Variables

C++ output is remarkably simple because you don't need format specifiers (like %d). cout automatically understands the type of the variable and formats it correctly for you.

Conclusion

Mastering cout and the << operator is the first interactive step in your C++ journey, allowing your programs to communicate their results and status to the world.
