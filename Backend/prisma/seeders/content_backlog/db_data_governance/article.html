Understanding Data Governance in Databases

Data governance is a collection of processes, roles, policies, standards, and metrics that ensure the effective and efficient use of information in enabling an organization to achieve its goals.

1. Data Quality and Standards

The core of governance is ensuring that data is **High Quality**, consistently defined across all systems, and adheres to the organization's business rules and formatting standards.

2. Compliance and Privacy

Governance is critical for complying with regulations like **GDPR**, **HIPAA**, and **CCPA**. It defines how data is stored, who can access it, and how it is deleted or anonymized when no longer needed.

3. Data Stewardship

Governance assigns clear roles (like **Data Stewards**) responsible for overseeing the data in different business segments, ensuring that information remains a valuable and secure corporate asset.

Conclusion

As organizations grow, formal data governance becomes essential to prevent data silos, ensure regulatory compliance, and build a culture of data-driven decision-making based on trusted information.
