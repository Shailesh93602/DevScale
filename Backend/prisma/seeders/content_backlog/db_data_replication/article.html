Understanding Data Replication

Data replication is the process of copying data from one database (the Primary) to one or more other databases (Replicas) to ensure they all contain the same information.

1. Why replicate data?

  • High Availability: If the primary database fails, a replica can take over immediately to prevent downtime.
  • Read Scaling: Offloading read-heavy queries to replicas, keeping the primary database free for faster writes.
  • Disaster Recovery: Keeping copies of the data in different geographical locations in case of a regional outage.

2. Replication Methods

Synchronous Replication: The primary database waits for a confirmation from the replica before committing a transaction. It's safer but slower. Asynchronous Replication: The primary commits immediately and sends data to replicas later. It's faster but has a small 'replication lag'.

3. Multi-Source/Circular Replication

More complex setups where data is replicated across multiple primary databases, allowing for even higher levels of redundancy and global performance.

Conclusion

Data replication is a core strategy for building world-class, resilient systems that can handle any scale and survive any catastrophic failure.
