Understanding Database Normalization

Database normalization is the process of organizing the columns and tables of a relational database to minimize data redundancy and improve data integrity.

1. The Goals of Normalization

The primary goals are to eliminate redundant data (storing the same data in more than one table) and to ensure data dependencies make sense (only storing related data in a table).

2. Normal Forms (NF)

  • 1NF (First Normal Form): Eliminate duplicate columns and ensure each cell contains a single (atomic) value.
  • 2NF (Second Normal Form): Meet all 1NF requirements and ensure that all non-key columns are fully dependent on the primary key.
  • 3NF (Third Normal Form): Meet all 2NF requirements and eliminate transitive dependencies (columns shouldn't depend on other non-key columns).

Conclusion

While over-normalization can sometimes lead to performance issues (due to more joins), following these principles is vital for building clean, maintainable, and reliable relational databases.
