Deep-Dive into Storage Engines: LSM-Trees vs. B-Trees

Every database is powered by a storage engine. To choose the right database, you must understand the two primary data structures that handle how bits are actually saved to the disk.

1. B-Trees (Read-Optimized)

Used by standard SQL databases (PostgreSQL, MySQL). B-Trees stay sorted on disk, making them incredibly fast for **Reads** and range queries. However, because every write requires updating the tree in multiple places, they are slower for high-velocity write workloads.

2. LSM-Trees (Write-Optimized)

Used by high-speed NoSQL databases (Cassandra, LevelDB). Log-Structured Merge (LSM) Trees treat all writes as append-only "Logs" in memory. They only periodically "Merge" these logs onto the disk. This makes **Writes** nearly instantaneous, but makes complex reads slightly slower as the system has to check multiple logs.

Conclusion

By mastering the storage engine deep-dive, you gain the ability to choose the perfect database for your specific workload. Use B-Trees for finance and reporting; use LSM-Trees for high-frequency trading and IoT data streams.
