Understanding Data Warehousing

A Data Warehouse is a centralized repository that stores integrated data from multiple disparate sources for reporting and data analysis.

1. OLTP vs. OLAP

  • OLTP (Online Transactional Processing): Optimized for fast write operations and daily business tasks (like a banking system).
  • OLAP (Online Analytical Processing): Optimized for complex queries and read-heavy data analysis (like a business intelligence tool).

2. The ETL Process

The core of data warehousing is ETL: Extract, Transform, Load. Data is extracted from various sources, transformed into a consistent format, and then loaded into the warehouse for analysis.

3. Business Intelligence (BI)

Data warehouses are the backbone of BI, allowing companies to run massive 'Big Data' queries to discover trends and make data-driven strategic decisions.

Conclusion

Data warehousing transforms raw operational data into actionable strategic insights, providing the foundation for analytical excellence in modern organizations.
