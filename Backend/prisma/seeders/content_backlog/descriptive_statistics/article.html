Understanding Descriptive Statistics

Using descriptive statistics correctly to summarize and analyze your project's data is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What are Descriptive Statistics?

Descriptive statistics are a set of mathematical tools—including its **Mean**, its **Median**, and its **Standard Deviation**—that providing the structure and tools for correctly summarizing your project's data as intended.

2. Why use Descriptive Statistics?

Descriptive statistics allow you to correctly track and manage exactly how your project's data is performing, identifying and fixing errors before they reach your users.

Conclusion

By making descriptive statistics a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
