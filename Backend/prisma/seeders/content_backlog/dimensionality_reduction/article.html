Understanding Dimensionality Reduction

Using **Dimensionality Reduction** correctly to simplify exactly how your project's data is handled—such as with **PCA (Principal Component Analysis)**—is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Dimensionality Reduction?

Dimensionality reduction is the process of correctly and securely reducing exactly how many features your project's data set has—while exactly how its most important information is preserved as intended.

2. Why use it?

Ideally, you should use dimensionality reduction to ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users, identifying exactly what part of its features are redundant correctly.

Conclusion

By making dimensionality reduction a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
