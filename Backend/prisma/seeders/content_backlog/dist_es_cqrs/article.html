Event Sourcing and CQRS Patterns

Standard databases store the *current state* of an object. Event Sourcing and CQRS take a different approach, storing every change that ever happened, providing total auditability and massive scalability.

1. Event Sourcing

Instead of storing a "Account Balance: $100", you store an append-only log of events: {Deposit: $50}, {Deposit: $70}, {Withdraw: $20}. To get the current state, you simply "replay" the events. This gives you a perfect audit trail and allows you to "travel back in time" to any state.

2. CQRS (Command Query Responsibility Segregation)

CQRS splits your application into two separate models: The **Command** side (Writing data) and the **Query** side (Reading data). By separating them, you can optimize them independently—for example, using a high-speed write database for commands and a read-optimized ElasticSearch index for complex queries.

3. Why Use Them?

These patterns are essential for high-compliance systems (banking, healthcare) and extremely high-traffic systems where the read and write loads are drastically different.

Conclusion

By mastering Event Sourcing and CQRS, you gain the ability to build massive, audit-proof systems that can handle hundreds of thousands of transactions per second on the world stage.
