Understanding Docker Registries

Using a Docker registry correctly to store and manage your project's container images is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is a Docker Registry?

A Docker registry is a central and secure repository for storing and managing your project's built Docker container images, ensuring they're available for your project's building and deployment.

2. Popular Docker Registries

Popular registries including **Docker Hub**, **Amazon ECR**, and **GitHub Container Registry** provide powerful, integrated, and feature-rich platforms for managing your containers correctly.

Conclusion

By making Docker registries a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
