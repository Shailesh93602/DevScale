Understanding Docker Volumes

Using Docker volumes correctly to manage your multi-container application's persistent data is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What are Docker Volumes?

Docker volumes are a custom status in your project's containers that allows for correctly handling exactly how your application's persistent data—such as its database—is stored and managed outside of its container.

2. Why use Docker Volumes?

Ideally, you should use Docker volumes to ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant even if its容器 is stopped or deleted.

3. Creating a Volume


docker volume create my-data
docker run -v my-data:/data my-app

Conclusion

By making Docker volumes a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
