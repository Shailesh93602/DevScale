Understanding Queues

A Queue is a linear data structure that follows the FIFO (First In, First Out) principle. Think of it like a line at the grocery store—the person who arrived first is serviced first.

1. Core Operations

  • Enqueue: Adds an item to the "Rear" of the queue.
  • Dequeue: Removes an item from the "Front" of the queue.
  • Front/Rear: Pointers to the start and end of the line.

2. Real-World Applications

Queues are vital for any system that handles shared resources. They power print queues, CPU task scheduling, and the "Message Queues" that allow different servers to communicate asynchronously.

Conclusion

Mastering Queues allows you to build systems that handle traffic and tasks fairly and efficiently. It's a foundational concept for networking, operating systems, and distributed computing.
