Understanding Stacks

A Stack is a linear data structure that follows the LIFO (Last In, First Out) principle. Think of it like a stack of plates—you can only add or remove from the very top.

1. Core Operations

  • Push: Adds an item to the top of the stack.
  • Pop: Removes the most recently added item from the top.
  • Peek/Top: Lets you look at the top item without removing it.

2. Real-World Applications

Stacks are everywhere in computer science. They power the "Undo" feature in your text editor, the "Back" button in your browser, and even how functions call other functions in memory (The Call Stack).

Conclusion

Stacks are simple but essential. By mastering this LIFO structure, you gain a vital tool for solving problems involving backtracking, expression evaluation, and memory management.
