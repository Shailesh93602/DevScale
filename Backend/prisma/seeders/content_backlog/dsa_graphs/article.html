Understanding Graphs

A graph is a non-linear data structure consisting of nodes (vertices) connected by edges. Graphs are extremely versatile and can represent everything from social networks to city infrastructure.

1. Terminology

A Vertex is a node in the graph, and an Edge is the connection between two vertices. Graphs can be Directed (edges have a direction) or Undirected.

2. Representation

Graphs are typically represented using an Adjacency Matrix (a 2D array) or an Adjacency List (an array of lists). Adjacency lists are generally more memory-efficient for sparse graphs.

3. Traversal Algorithms

The two primary ways to explore a graph are Depth-First Search (DFS), which uses a stack or recursion, and Breadth-First Search (BFS), which uses a queue to visit neighbors layer-by-layer.

Conclusion

Mastering graphs is essential for solving complex problems like finding the shortest path, detecting cycles, or analyzing networked data.
