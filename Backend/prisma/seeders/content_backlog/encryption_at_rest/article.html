Understanding Data Encryption at Rest

Using **Data Encryption at Rest** correctly to protect your application's stored data—such as in its **Database** or its **Files**—is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Encryption at Rest?

Data encryption at rest is the process of correctly and securely encrypting exactly how your project's data is stored on physical media as intended, identifying and fixing security vulnerabilities.

2. Important Factors

Encryption at rest helps to ensure your software is accurate, performant, and reliable, protecting your application's most valuable asset—its data—from unauthorized physical access correctly as intended.

Conclusion

By making encryption at rest a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
