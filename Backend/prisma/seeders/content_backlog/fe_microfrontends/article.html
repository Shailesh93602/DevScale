Modern Frontend: Micro-Frontends and Module Federation

As enterprise frontends grow, they become too large for a single team. Micro-frontends allow you to split a massive website into independent, deployable pieces, just like microservices for the backend.

1. What is a Micro-Frontend?

A Micro-Frontend is a self-contained feature (e.g., the "Checkout" or "Search" bar) that is built, tested, and deployed by its own team. It can even be written in a different framework (React, Vue, or Angular) while still appearing as a single, seamless app to the user.

2. Module Federation (webpack 5)

Module Federation is the technology that makes micro-frontends practical. It allowed different builds to share code and load each other's components dynamically at **Runtime**. This means you can update the "Header" of your site without redeploying the "Main Content," drastically increasing deployment speed.

Conclusion

Mastering Micro-frontends allows you to scale your frontend organization to hundreds of developers. You eliminate the "Monolithic Frontend" bottleneck and enable a culture of independent, high-velocity product delivery.
