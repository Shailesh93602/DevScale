FullStack Design Patterns

Design patterns in a FullStack context involve organizing both the client and server codebases to ensure scalability, maintainability, and clear separation of concerns. From MVC to Microfrontends, choosing the right pattern is key to a project's success.

1. MVC (Model-View-Controller)

The classic MVC pattern is fundamental: The Model manages data and business logic, the View handles the UI, and the Controller mediates between them. In modern FullStack, your backend acts as the Model/Controller, while the frontend is the View.

2. Component-Based Architecture

On the frontend, leveraging a component-based approach (like in React or Vue) allows you to build encapsulated, reusable UI pieces. This pattern fits perfectly with Atomic Design principles.

3. Repository and Service Patterns

Your backend can benefit from the Repository Pattern to abstract data access and the Service Pattern to house complex business logic, keeping your controllers thin and readable.

Conclusion

Applying consistent design patterns across your fullstack application reduces technical debt and makes the codebase easier for team members to navigate and contribute to.
