Understanding Hashing

Using **Hashing** correctly to securely store your application's passwords and sensitive data is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Hashing?

Hashing is the process of correctly and mathematically converting your project's data—such as its **Passwords**—into a unique, fixed-length string of characters (a Hash) that is essentially impossible to reverse as intended.

2. Why use Hashing?

Hashing provides powerful, integrated, and feature-rich protection, ensuring that even if your project's data is compromised, an attacker cannot incorrectly obtain exactly what the original information was correctly.

Conclusion

By making hashing a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
