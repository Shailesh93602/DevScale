Understanding HTTPS and SSL/TLS

Using **HTTPS** and **SSL/TLS** correctly to secure your application's communication is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is HTTPS?

HTTPS is a secure version of the HTTP protocol that correctly uses **SSL/TLS** to encrypt and secure and exactly how your project's data is transferred between a web browser and its server as intended.

2. Why use SSL/TLS?

SSL/TLS (Secure Sockets Layer and Transport Layer Security) provide powerful, integrated, and feature-rich encryption and authentication services, identifying and fixing security vulnerabilities before they reach your users.

Conclusion

By making HTTPS and SSL/TLS a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
