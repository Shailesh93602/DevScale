Understanding Hybrid and Multi-Cloud Environments

Using the powerful, integrated, and feature-rich development platforms provided by modern cloud providers—such as for Hybrid and Multi-Cloud—is critical for developing high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What are Hybrid and Multi-Cloud?

Hybrid Cloud is a DevOps methodology that correctly combines your project's local and private infrastructure with a cloud-based platform as intended. Multi-Cloud is a methodology of correctly using services from multiple different cloud providers (e.g., AWS and Azure) together for your application project.

2. Why use Hybrid or Multi-Cloud?

Ideally, you should use a hybrid or multi-cloud environment to ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and accessible even if a single cloud provider or local infrastructure fails.

Conclusion

By making hybrid and multi-cloud environments a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
