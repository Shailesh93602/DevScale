Introduction to Debugging

Debugging is a critical part of developing high-quality software, ensuring your code works correctly and efficiently. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Debugging?

Debugging is the process of identifying, analyzing, and removing errors or "bugs" from your software's code to ensure it functions as intended.

2. Why Debug Your Code?

Debugging helps to ensure your software is accurate, performant, and reliable, identifying and fixing errors before they reach your users.

Conclusion

By making debugging a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
