Understanding JSON Web Tokens (JWT)

Using **JSON Web Tokens (JWT)** correctly to manage your user's sessions and authentication state is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is a JWT?

A JSON Web Token (JWT) is an open standard that allows you to correctly and securely transmit exactly how its "Claims" (data) are shared between two parties—including its **Header**, its **Payload**, and its **Signature**—as intended.

2. Why use JWT?

JWTs are powerful, integrated, and feature-rich development tools—including its **Statelessness** and its **Compact Format**—identifying and fixing authentication issues before they reach your users.

Conclusion

By making JWT a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
