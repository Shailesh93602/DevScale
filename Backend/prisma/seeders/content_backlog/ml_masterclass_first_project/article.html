Building Your First ML Project

A practical project is the best way to understand the challenges of real-world Machine Learning. We'll walk through building a simple predictive model, such as a Housing Price Predictor or an Iris Species Classifier.

1. Exploring the Data

Use Pandas to load your data and Matplotlib or Seaborn for exploratory data analysis (EDA). Understanding the distributions and correlations in your data is crucial for choosing the right model.

2. Training and Testing

Always split your data into Training and Testing sets. This ensures that you can evaluate how well your model generalizes to new, unseen data, preventing Overfitting.

3. Performance Metrics

Choose the right metrics for your task. For regression, use Mean Squared Error (MSE). For classification, look at Accuracy, Precision, and Recall.

Conclusion

Your first project won't be perfect, and that's okay. The goal is to work through the entire pipeline and understand how data and model choices affect final predictions.
