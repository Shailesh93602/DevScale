Understanding Mocking and Spying in Jest

Using the powerful mocking and spying capabilities provided by Jest, you can create a suite of unit tests for your project. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Mocking?

Mocking is a technique where you replace a real component of your code with a "fake" or "mock" version, allowing you to isolate the unit you're testing and control the behavior of its dependencies.

2. Creating a Mock Function


const mockFn = jest.fn();
mockFn('arg');
expect(mockFn).toHaveBeenCalledWith('arg');

3. What is Spying?

Spying is a technique where you "spy" on a real component of your code, recording its behavior without changing it.

4. Creating a Spy


const spy = jest.spyOn(console, 'log');
console.log('test');
expect(spy).toHaveBeenCalledWith('test');

Conclusion

By making unit testing a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
