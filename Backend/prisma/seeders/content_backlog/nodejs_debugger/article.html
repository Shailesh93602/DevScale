Understanding the Node.js Debugger

Using the built-in and powerful Node.js Debugger is critical for developing high-quality server-side JavaScript applications. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is the Node.js Debugger?

The Node.js Debugger is a built-in debugging utility for Node.js applications, allowing you to pause, inspect, and step through your server-side code directly in your project's terminal or even a browser.

2. Starting the Debugger


node inspect index.js

3. Choosing a Debugging Strategy

Choose a debugging strategy that works for you and your team, such as using the Node.js Debugger to identify the cause of complex server-side logic errors or profiling your server-side application's performance.

Conclusion

By making the Node.js Debugger a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
