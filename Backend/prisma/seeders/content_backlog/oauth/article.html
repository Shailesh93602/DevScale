Understanding OAuth Implementation

Using **OAuth** correctly to allow your users to authorize their data from other services—such as **Google** or **GitHub**—is critical for maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is OAuth?

OAuth is an open standard that allows you to correctly and securely provide exactly how a third-party application can access your project's data—without its owner giving away its password—as intended.

2. Why use OAuth?

OAuth helps to ensure your software is accurate, performant, and reliable, identifying and fixing security vulnerabilities—such as **Credential Theft**—before they reach your users.

Conclusion

By making OAuth implementation a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
