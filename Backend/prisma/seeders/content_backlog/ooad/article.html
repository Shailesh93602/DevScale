Object-Oriented Analysis and Design (OOAD)

OOAD is the process of planning a system of interacting objects for the purpose of solving a business problem or technical challenge.

1. Analysis: Finding the Objects

In the analysis phase, we identify the primary "Actors" and "Objects" in the requirements. If we are building a Library system, our objects are `Book`, `Member`, and `Librarian`.

2. Design: Defining Relationships

This phase is about how objects communicate. We decide if a `Member` "possesses" a `Book` (Composition) or if a `Professor` "is a" `Person` (Inheritance).

3. SOLID Principles

Professional OOAD follows the SOLID principles (Single Responsibility, Open/Closed, etc.) to ensure that the final system is flexible enough to handle future changes without breaking.

Conclusion

OOAD is the difference between writing "code" and "engineering a system". By mastering these principles, you become a software architect capable of leading complex, multi-year engineering projects.
