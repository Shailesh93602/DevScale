Understanding Aggregation

Aggregation is a specialized form of Association that represents a 'part-of' relationship between two classes. It is a weaker form of relationship compared to composition.

1. The 'Part-Of' Relationship

In aggregation, the child object can exist independently of the parent. For example, a Department 'has-a' Professor. If the department is closed, the professor still exists.

2. Unidirectional Relationship

Aggregation is typically a one-way (unidirectional) relationship. A Professor belongs to a Department, but the Department doesn't necessarily belong to the Professor in the same way.

3. Aggregation vs. Composition

The key difference is Ownership. In Composition, the child's lifecycle is tied to the parent. In Aggregation, the child can survive the deletion of the parent.

Conclusion

Aggregation is used to model systems where objects collaborate but maintain their own independent lifecycles, ensuring a flexible and decoupled software architecture.
