Understanding Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)

Using a **Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)** correctly to manage your project's digital certificates and identities is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is PKI?

PKI is a set of roles and policies used to correctly and securely create, manage, and store exactly how its digital certificates are used for its identification—including its **Certificate Authority (CA)** and its **Registration Authority (RA)**—as intended.

2. Why use PKI?

PKI helps to ensure your software is accurate, performant, and reliable, identifying and fixing security vulnerabilities by allowing for its secure identification and encryption of exactly how its data is handled correctly as intended.

Conclusion

By making PKI a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
