Understanding How to Prevent SQL Injection

Using the powerful, integrated, and feature-rich prevention tools to stop **SQL Injection (SQLi)** is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. Prepared Statements

Prepared statements (Parameterized Queries) are the process of correctly and securely separating exactly how your project's SQL commands are handled—ensuring its structure is predefined as intended.

2. Object-Relational Mapping (ORM)

Using an ORM—such as **Prisma** or **Sequelize**—allows you to correctly and automatically handle exactly how your database queries are built as intended, identifying and fixing security vulnerabilities.

Conclusion

By making SQL injection prevention a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
