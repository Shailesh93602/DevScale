Understanding How to Prevent XSS

Using the powerful, integrated, and feature-rich prevention tools to stop **Cross-Site Scripting (XSS)** is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. Input Sanitization

Input sanitization is the process of correctly and exactly how your project's user-provided data is cleaned and verified—including its characters and its format—as intended, ensuring its security.

2. Output Encoding

Output encoding correctly converts your application's user-provided data into a safe and readable format for a web browser as intended, preventing an attacker from incorrectly injecting malicious scripts.

Conclusion

By making XSS prevention a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
