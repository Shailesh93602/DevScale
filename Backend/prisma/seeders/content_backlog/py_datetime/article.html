Mastering Date and Time in Python

Managing dates, times, and timezones is a common requirement for everything from logging events to scheduling database backups. Python's datetime module is the standard choice for these tasks.

1. Core Classes

  • date : Stores year, month, and day.
  • time : Stores hour, minute, second, and microsecond.
  • datetime : Combines both date and time into a single object.

2. Formatting with strftime

To display dates in a human-readable way (e.g., "Monday, Jan 1st"), you use the strftime() method with special format codes like %B for the full month name and %Y for the year.

3. Timedelta and Arithmetic

The timedelta class allows you to perform "Date Arithmetic". You can easily calculate what the date will be 30 days from now, or find the difference between two timestamps in seconds.

Conclusion

Working with time can be complex due to leap years and timezones, but Python's built-in tools provide a reliable and consistent way to handle these complications in your apps.
