Mastering Python Logging

Logging is a means of tracking events that happen when some software runs. For professional applications, print() is almost never enough; you need a persistent, structured track record of what your program is doing.

1. Log Levels

Python's logging module defines different levels of importance for logs: DEBUG, INFO, WARNING, ERROR, and CRITICAL. You can configure your app to only show logs above a certain level.

2. Why not print()?

Logging allows you to send output to different places simultaneously—like the console, a file on the server, or even a remote monitoring service. It's safe for production and provides much more context (like timestamps and module names).

3. Log Formatting

You can customize exactly how your logs look: %(asctime)s - %(name)s - %(levelname)s - %(message)s. This makes it incredibly easy to search and analyze logs when a bug occurs in production.

Conclusion

By professionalizing your output with the logging module, you gain the visibility needed to debug complex, real-world systems that run across multiple servers and handle thousands of users.
