Mastering Python Loops (For and While)

Loops allow you to execute a block of code multiple times, which is essential for processing collections, recurring logic, and building complex algorithms.

1. The for Loop (Iteration)

In Python, the for loop is used for iterating over a sequence (such as a list, tuple, or string). It works more like an 'iterator' method found in other object-oriented languages: for x in my_list:.

2. The while Loop (Condition-based)

A while loop executes as long as a certain condition remains True. This is ideal when you don't know exactly how many times you need to repeat—like waiting for a specific user input.

3. break and continue

  • break : Immediately exits the loop, regardless of the condition.
  • continue : Skips the current iteration and jumps directly to the beginning of the next one.

Conclusion

Loops are the engine of your code. By mastering both iteration and condition-based repeating, you gain the power to automate repetitive tasks and process massive amounts of data efficiently.
