Understanding Python Tuples

A tuple is a collection that is Ordered and Unchangeable (Immutable). They are written with round brackets: my_tuple = ("apple", "banana").

1. Immutability: The Safety Feature

Once a tuple is created, you cannot change its values, add new items, or remove existing ones. This makes tuples perfect for data that should never be altered, like coordinates or database record structures.

2. Efficiency and Speed

Because tuples have a fixed size and content, they are slightly faster and more memory-efficient than lists. They also provide a level of 'protection' against accidental modifications in large projects.

3. Packing and Unpacking

Python allows you to "pack" variables into a tuple and "unpack" them back into separate variables easily: x, y = my_tuple. This is the foundation of returning multiple values from a function.

Conclusion

Tuples are the ideal structure for data integrity. Use them whenever you have a collection of items that should remain constant throughout the lifetime of your application.
