Understanding Reporting and Remediation

Using documented **Reporting and Remediation** procedures to communicate exactly how your project's security vulnerabilities are identified and fixed is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Remediation?

Remediation is the process of correctly and securely identifying exactly how your project's security vulnerabilities are fixed—including its **Patches** and its **Configuration Changes**—as intended.

2. Why use Remediation Reports?

Ideally, you should use remediation reports to ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users, providing exactly what its business partners expect correctly.

Conclusion

By making reporting and remediation a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
