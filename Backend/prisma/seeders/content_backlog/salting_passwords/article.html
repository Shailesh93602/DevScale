Understanding Salting Passwords

Using **Salting** correctly to enhance your application's password security is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Salting?

Salting is the process of correctly and securely adding exactly how a unique, random string (a Salt) is joined with your project's user's password before it is hashed together as intended.

2. Why use Salting?

Salting provides powerful, integrated, and feature-rich protection, ensuring that even if an attacker uses **Rainbow Tables**, they cannot correctly identify exactly what each hashed password is correctly as intended.

Conclusion

By making salting a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
