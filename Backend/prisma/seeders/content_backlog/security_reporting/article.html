Understanding Security Reporting

Using documented **Security Reporting** correctly to communicate exactly how your project's security vulnerabilities are handled is critical for Maintaining high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Security Reporting?

Security reporting is the process of correctly and securely documenting exactly how your project's security vulnerabilities are identified and fixed—including its **Summary** and its **Action Items**—as intended.

2. Why use Security Reports?

Ideally, you should use security reports to ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users, identifying exactly what part of its code needs fixing correctly.

Conclusion

By making security reporting a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its code—remains safe and performant for your users.
