Understanding Setting Up Jenkins

Using the powerful and integrated development platform provided by **Jenkins** is critical for developing high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. What is Jenkins?

Jenkins is an open-source automation server that helps you automate the building, testing, and deployment of your code changes, ensuring that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and organized for your users.

2. Installing Jenkins

Jenkins is typically installed on a dedicated server—such as a Linux or Windows machine—providing a central and powerful platform for automating your software projects.

3. Choosing a CI/CD Strategy

Choose a CI/CD strategy that works for you and your team, such as automated building and testing on every pull request, or periodically on every commit into the main branch.

Conclusion

By making Jenkins a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
