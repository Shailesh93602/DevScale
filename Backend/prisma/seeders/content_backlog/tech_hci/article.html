The Ethics of HCI: Accessibility and UX

Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) is the study of how people use technology. For a professional engineer, this means designing systems that are not just "Functional," but accessible to everyone on the planet.

1. WCAG 2.1 Compliance

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) are the international law for digital accessibility. Professional apps must ensure proper **Color Contrast**, **Keyboard Navigation** (no mouse required), and **Screen Reader compatibility** using ARIA labels.

2. Cognitive Load in UX

UX Design for developers is about reducing "Cognitive Load." Every extra click, every confusing icon, and every long form increases the user's frustration. We use principles like **Fitts's Law** and **Hick's Law** to build interfaces that feel intuitive and "Invisible."

Conclusion

Mastering HCI and Accessibility transforms you from a "Coder" into an "Inclusive Engineer." It ensures that your technology can be used by anyone, regardless of their physical abilities or technical background.
