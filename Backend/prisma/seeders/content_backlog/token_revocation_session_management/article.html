Understanding Revoking Tokens and Session Management

Using the powerful, integrated, and feature-rich token and session management tools—such as a **Token Blacklist**—is critical for Developing high-quality software. By following these steps, you can set it up in minutes.

1. Why revoke Tokens?

Revoking tokens correctly allows you to correctly track and manage exactly how your project's authenticated user's access is removed—identifying and fixing security vulnerabilities—such as **Stolen Tokens**—before they reach your users.

2. Token Blacklisting

Ideally, you should maintain a "Blacklist" of revoked tokens—such as in a **Redis** cache—to correctly verify that exactly how each incoming request's token behaves as intended, ensuring its security.

Conclusion

By making token and session revocation a priority, you can ensure that your application's most valuable asset—its data—remains safe and performant for your users.
