Frontend Frameworks Best Practices

Building a project with a frontend framework is only half the battle. To ensure that your application remains maintainable, accessible, and fast, you must follow established industry best practices that scale with your team and your codebase.

1. Maintain Consistent Spacing and Colors

The most common mistake in framework usage is "drifting" away from your standard theme by adding ad-hoc custom styles (e.g. margin-top: 17px; instead of using .mt-4). Always strictly follow your design system's spacing, color, and font scales to maintain a professional look and feel.

2. Accessibility is Not Optional

  • Sufficient Color Contrast: Ensure that your text remains readable for all users, including those with visual impairments.
  • Aria Labels and Roles: Use standard HTML5 semantic tags and add aria-label or aria-describedby to complex interactive components (like Modals or Dropdowns) to ensure they work with screen readers.
  • Keyboard Navigation: Test that all buttons, links, and forms can be fully navigated and used with only a keyboard.

3. Component Composition

Instead of copying a giant block of 20 Tailwind classes for every "Button" across your app, encapsulate that style in a single reusable component (like PrimaryButton.vue or AppButton.tsx). This makes it much easier to update your entire app's design by changing just one file.

Conclusion

By following these frontend framework best practices, you're transitioning from a student to a true frontend engineer. Whether you're optimizing for accessibility, consistency, or maintainability, these skills are the secret to building high-quality and professional user interfaces for your users.
