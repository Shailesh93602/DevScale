Data Governance Frameworks

Without deliberate governance, data in a large organization becomes chaotic. Different teams call the same metric different things — the Sales team calls it "Revenue," the Finance team calls it "Bookings," and the Product team calls it "GMV." Each team independently calculates it using a slightly different SQL formula, resulting in embarrassing discrepancies when three different numbers are presented in the same board meeting.

A Data Governance Framework is the organizational structure, processes, and technology that establishes clear accountability, standardized definitions, and enforceable policies around data assets.

Core Components of Data Governance

  • Data Governance Committee: A cross-functional group of senior stakeholders (CTO, Head of Analytics, Legal, Compliance) who set the overall data strategy, prioritize governance initiatives, and resolve cross-departmental data disputes.
  • Data Owners: Business executives accountable for the quality, accuracy, and policy compliance of a specific data domain. The Head of Finance is the Data Owner for all financial data.
  • Data Stewards: Technical practitioners (Data Engineers, Analysts) responsible for the day-to-day execution of governance policies — implementing the data quality tests, maintaining the Data Catalog, and enforcing masking policies.

The Business Glossary

The Business Glossary is the foundation of effective governance. It is a centralized, curated dictionary of canonical business term definitions, owned by the Data Governance Committee.

The Data Catalog

A Data Catalog (like Apache Atlas, DataHub, or Collibra) is the searchable technical directory of all data assets in an organization. It links technical metadata (table schemas, column types, last update times) with human-readable business context (descriptions, glossary links, data owners, sensitivity classifications).

When a new Data Analyst joins the company, instead of asking 10 different people "which table has customer data?", they search the Data Catalog and immediately find the authoritative dim_customers table, its schema documentation, its owner, and a link to its lineage graph.

The DAMA-DMBOK Framework

The DAMA Data Management Body of Knowledge (DMBOK) is the industry-standard reference framework defining 11 Data Management knowledge areas, including Data Quality, Data Security, Master Data Management, and Metadata Management. Most enterprise data governance programs are structured around DAMA-DMBOK principles.
