CSS Typography: The Voice of Your Content

Typography is the art of arranging and styling text. On the web, where reading is the primary activity, mastering typography is essential for providing a high-quality user experience.

Essential Font Properties

  • font-family: Selects the font to use (e.g., 'Inter', sans-serif). Always provide a generic "fallback" like serif or sans-serif.
  • font-size: Sets the size of the text. (Best practice is to use rem units for accessibility).
  • font-weight: Controls the thickness of the font (e.g., 400 for normal, 700 for bold).
  • line-height: Sets the vertical space between lines of text. A value of 1.5 or 1.6 is usually best for readability.

Text Alignment and Spacing

  • text-align: Aligns text left, center, right, or justified.
  • letter-spacing: Sets the space between individual letters.
  • word-spacing: Sets the space between words.

Text Decoration and Transformation

  • text-decoration: Adds underlines, overlines, or strikethroughs.
  • text-transform: Automatically changes text to uppercase, lowercase, or capitalized (e.g., a "Buy Now" button).

Using Google Fonts and @font-face

You aren't limited to "system fonts" like Arial or Times New Roman. You can use services like **Google Fonts** to import thousands of beautiful custom typefaces into your project using the @font-face rule or the <link> tag.

💡 Pro Tip: For the best readability, limit your "line length" (the width of your text block) to around 60-80 characters per line. This prevents the user's eye from getting tired while reading long articles.
